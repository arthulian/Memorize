# Ordo Amoris vs. Cupiditas

---

## 1. Relationship to Truth and Thinking

### Ordo Amoris
#### Aim: Seeks truth in order to gain wisdom.
#### Method: Revises beliefs when presented with evidence.

### Cupiditas
#### Aim: Uses logic to dominate rather than understand.
#### Method: Dismisses opposing views instead of engaging them.

---

## 2. Sense-Making and Understanding

### Ordo Amoris
#### Aim: Aims for deeper, more accurate understanding.
#### Method: Integrates complexity rather than reducing it.

### Cupiditas
#### Aim: Seeks control over meaning.
#### Method: Forces data into oversimplified narratives.

---

## 3. Objectivity and Assumptions

### Ordo Amoris
#### Aim: Pursues genuine impartiality.
#### Method: Tests and questions its own assumptions.

### Cupiditas
#### Aim: Preserves bias or avoids responsibility.
#### Method: Pretends neutrality to justify bias or inaction.

---

## 4. Self-Control and Emotion

### Ordo Amoris
#### Aim: Seeks freedom and creative capacity.
#### Method: Masters impulses rather than suppressing them.

### Cupiditas
#### Aim: Seeks dominance and emotional containment.
#### Method: Suppresses emotions rigidly and controls others through discipline.

---

## 5. Fairness and Justice

### Ordo Amoris
#### Aim: Upholds justice as a moral good.
#### Method: Balances empathy with accountability.

### Cupiditas
#### Aim: Advances personal advantage.
#### Method: Applies rules inconsistently for personal gain.

---

## 6. Perseverance and Effort

### Ordo Amoris
#### Aim: Moves toward meaningful goals.
#### Method: Adapts resiliently when conditions change.

### Cupiditas
#### Aim: Protects pride and self-image.
#### Method: Clings stubbornly to lost causes.

---

## 7. Self-Discipline and Alignment

### Ordo Amoris
#### Aim: Lives in alignment with values.
#### Method: Adjusts actions mindfully when misaligned.

### Cupiditas
#### Aim: Seeks control through perfection.
#### Method: Obsessively pursues perfection at the expense of well-being.

---

## 8. Self-Efficacy and Confidence

### Ordo Amoris
#### Aim: Serves and empowers others.
#### Method: Builds confidence through competence and contribution.

### Cupiditas
#### Aim: Asserts superiority and dominance.
#### Method: Dismisses feedback and correction.

---

## 9. Temperance and Energy Use

### Ordo Amoris
#### Aim: Honors priorities and long-term purpose.
#### Method: Balances energy intentionally.

### Cupiditas
#### Aim: Avoids effort or responsibility.
#### Method: Uses temperance to justify laziness or avoidance.

---

## 10. Self-Reflection and Inner Life

### Ordo Amoris
#### Aim: Grows in wisdom and compassion.
#### Method: Reflects honestly on experience.

### Cupiditas
#### Aim: Protects the ego from discomfort.
#### Method: Ruminates on shame or justifies selfishness.

---

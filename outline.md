# Dialectic of Love: Rightly Ordered vs. Disordered

## 1. Critical Thinking
   - **Rightly Ordered Love**
     - **Loves:** Truth
     - **Expression:**
       - Evaluates evidence fairly
       - Embraces nuance
     - *Example:* "This claim is partially true, but only under these conditions."

   - **Disordered Love**
     - **Loves:** Being "right" more than truth
     - **Expression:**
       - Dismisses evidence
       - Overcomplicates or oversimplifies
     - *Example:* "I refuse to accept any evidence that contradicts my view."

---

## 2. Sense-Making
   - **Rightly Ordered Love**
     - **Loves:** Clarity
     - **Expression:**
       - Seeks to integrate diverse perspectives
       - Creates a coherent whole
     - *Example:* "This data seems conflicting, but here’s how it fits together."

   - **Disordered Love**
     - **Loves:** Simplicity or complexity for its own sake
     - **Expression:**
       - Ignores contradictions
       - Forces connections
     - *Example:* "I’ll just pick the easiest explanation, even if it doesn’t fit all the facts."

---

## 3. Objectivity
   - **Rightly Ordered Love**
     - **Loves:** Fairness
     - **Expression:**
       - Strives to see all sides impartially
       - Applies standards uniformly
     - *Example:* "Let me consider how I’d judge this if I were in their shoes."

   - **Disordered Love**
     - **Loves:** Detachment or cynicism
     - **Expression:**
       - Ignores legitimate emotions
       - Dismisses moral considerations
     - *Example:* "Feelings don’t matter—only cold, hard facts do."

---

## 4. Self-Control
   - **Rightly Ordered Love**
     - **Loves:** Integrity
     - **Expression:**
       - Aligns actions with values
       - Manages impulses with purpose
     - *Example:* "I’ll wait to respond until I’ve calmed down."

   - **Disordered Love**
     - **Loves:** Repression or indulgence
     - **Expression:**
       - Denies all desires **or**
       - Gives in to every impulse
     - *Example:* "I deserve this now, and I don’t care about the consequences."

---

## 5. Fairness
   - **Rightly Ordered Love**
     - **Loves:** Justice
     - **Expression:**
       - Holds everyone accountable
       - Applies rules consistently
     - *Example:* "I made a mistake, and I’ll own up to it."

   - **Disordered Love**
     - **Loves:** Favoritism or fault-finding
     - **Expression:**
       - Applies rules inconsistently
       - Makes exceptions for self or others arbitrarily
     - *Example:* "Rules are for other people, not for me."

---

## 6. Perseverance
   - **Rightly Ordered Love**
     - **Loves:** Growth
     - **Expression:**
       - Persists through challenges with reason
       - Adapts approach as needed
     - *Example:* "This is tough, but I’ll keep trying and adjust my approach if needed."

   - **Disordered Love**
     - **Loves:** Obstinacy or quitting
     - **Expression:**
       - Refuses to stop even when harmful **or**
       - Gives up at the first sign of difficulty
     - *Example:* "I’ll keep doing the same thing, even though it’s not working."

---

## 7. Self-Discipline
   - **Rightly Ordered Love**
     - **Loves:** Consistency
     - **Expression:**
       - Balances effort with self-compassion
       - Pursues progress incrementally
     - *Example:* "I’ll work on this a little each day, even if I don’t feel like it."

   - **Disordered Love**
     - **Loves:** Rigidity or laxity
     - **Expression:**
       - Burns out from over-discipline **or**
       - Never starts due to perfectionism
     - *Example:* "I’ll do it perfectly or not at all."

---

## 8. Self-Efficacy
   - **Rightly Ordered Love**
     - **Loves:** Confidence rooted in competence
     - **Expression:**
       - Learns from setbacks
       - Recognizes real progress
     - *Example:* "I’ve handled challenges like this before, and I can do it again."

   - **Disordered Love**
     - **Loves:** Arrogance or self-doubt
     - **Expression:**
       - Overestimates abilities **or**
       - Dismisses all progress
     - *Example:* "I’ll never be good enough, so why try?"

---

## 9. Temperance
   - **Rightly Ordered Love**
     - **Loves:** Balance
     - **Expression:**
       - Uses resources wisely
       - Prioritizes sustainability
     - *Example:* "I’ll give my best effort to what matters most and rest when needed."

   - **Disordered Love**
     - **Loves:** Slacking or overworking
     - **Expression:**
       - Wastes energy **or**
       - Never rests
     - *Example:* "I’ll work until I collapse—sleep is for the weak."

---

## 10. Self-Reflection
   - **Rightly Ordered Love**
     - **Loves:** Wisdom
     - **Expression:**
       - Turns experiences into growth
       - Asks constructive questions
     - *Example:* "What can I learn from this to do better next time?"

   - **Disordered Love**
     - **Loves:** Rumination or avoidance
     - **Expression:**
       - Obsesses over mistakes **or**
       - Never reflects on actions
     - *Example:* "I’m a failure, and I’ll never change."

---

| Self-Efficacy       | Loves confidence; learns from setbacks.          | Loves arrogance/self-doubt.                  |
| Temperance          | Loves balance; uses resources wisely.            | Loves slacking/overworking.                  |
